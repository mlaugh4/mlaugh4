Ajua is a research technology company out of MIT that connects health researchers and businesses with populations in hard-to-reach areas using SMS. After spending a summer in Nairobi, I continued to provide design and front-end development support during my second year of grad school.

The techincal expectations were much greater than anticipated, so I spent the first month focused on research and design, while spending evenings studying front-end languages, frameworks, and best practices. In the second half of my internship I was spending most of my time on interface development and battling d3.

I ultimately worked on a product called Consumer Wallet that measured Kenya's cash-based economy in real time that was covered in Forbes, 2017. 

[Forbes article](https://www.forbes.com/sites/montymunford/2017/05/03/msurvey-and-safaricom-continue-kenyas-mobile-money-revolution/#555cc62d133d)
